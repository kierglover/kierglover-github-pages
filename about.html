Find out more about my journey into design and entrepreneurship and how I got to where I am today.

Since an early age I have always been a creative and practical person. Through my teenage years I turned my hand to music, drama, writing and even some illustration. Alongside this, I was always interested in how things work. However, I didn't make the connection between design and technology until later on.

After initially going into University as an Drama and English student, the practical side of me quickly realised that this wasn't the best allocation of my money and time. I wanted something broader, so I settled on Business Management. Soon after starting my Business Management degree, I got very interested in digital marketing and understanding what makes customers tick.

Fast forwarding somewhat and several interviews later my first internship was as a digital marketing assistant for a London based startup. Working round a dining room table, bashing out blog posts and constantly analysing Google Analytics trends with 5 others people was a tough but valuable experience.

Despite my experience in London my creative urges only grew stronger. So I decided to go back to University to study a Masters in Digital Media. This is when it all clicked. I became obsessed with coding websites, designing interfaces for apps and generating business ideas. The rest is history. I went 100% freelance in early 2016 and am constantly searching for new, exciting projects to help grow my career. In the summer of 2017 I am moving to Phnom Penh Cambodia to combine my entrepreneurial spirit, technology and passion to make a difference in a place that's dear to my heart.
